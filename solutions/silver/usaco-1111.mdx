## Explanation

One thing that is clear from the problem statement is that for 
Bessie to visit an ancestor, she must visit the preceding year of 
the Ox and must wait at least $12$ years to reach them. Based on 
this, we can convert each ancestor's year into its $12$-year cycle 
$\lfloor{\frac{year + 11}{12}}\rfloor$. 

Now, we must use one jump to travel back to the earliest cycle, 
leaving us with $k - 1$ jumps. With these remaining jumps, it is 
optimal to skip the $k - 1$ largest gaps between consecutive 
cycles in the sorted array, since **skipping a larger gap saves more time**. 

We will then greedily subtract these $k - 1$ largest gaps from the 
total cycle count. Finally, we multiply our result by 12 to 
convert back into years, giving us the minimum time for Bessie to 
visit all her ancestors and return to the present.
